Solar Eclipse

A solar eclipse is an event when the shadow of the Moon block a part of sunlight. This occur when Sun, Moon and Earth are aligned in a way. It is a completely natural phenomena.

The position of the Moon results in different types of Solar Eclipse. They are:-

  1. Total Solar Eclipse - It occurs when the shadow of the Moon completely block the sunlight to reach the Earth.


  2. Annular Solar Eclipse - It occurs when the Moon and the Sun are exactly line with Earth. Since the size of Moon is smaller than the Sun, it cannot covers entirely here. So a bright ring or annulus appears with dark at centre due to the Moon.


  3. Partial Solar Eclipse - It occurs when the Moon block only a part of the Sun. This phenomeon also occurs during total or annular solar eclipse where full eclipse is not visible.


  4. Hybrid Solar Eclipse - The most rare type of Solar Eclipse where at certain place Annular Solar Eclipse is visible while at other points it appear Total Eclipse.

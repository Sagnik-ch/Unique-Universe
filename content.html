Looking at the wonders......


Galaxies are gravitational bound system consist of compact stars ( a term used collectively for white, dwarf, neutron stars and black hole), interstellar dust and gases. They are formed after the Big Bang and are classified by their visual appearance like Eliptical, Spiral, Shell, Barred Spiral etc. There is a common for many galaxies to have a supermassive black hole at center.

Our galaxy "MILKY WAY", sometimes called Akash Ganga consist of our solar system. It is a spiral galaxy with bar-shaped structure at center composed of billions of stars. Hence, Milky Way is classified as barred spiral galaxy. It has spiral arms composed of gases. The galactic center has a super massive blackhole called Sagittarius A.

Galileo Galilei proof it in 1610 with his telescope and discovered it is made of hugh numbers of faint stars.

In our local group it is the second largest galaxy after Andromeda. A group of more than 20 galaxies are categorized as a local group. Recent researchs suggested it began shortly after Big Bang.
